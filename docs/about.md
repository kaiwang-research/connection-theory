# About Connection Theory

Connection Theory is an independent long-term research project exploring the fundamental principles that govern persistent adaptive systems.

The project seeks to develop a unified framework for understanding organization, adaptation, intelligence, and long-term persistence across natural and artificial systems.



## Mission

Our mission is to develop a unified conceptual framework capable of describing adaptive systems across multiple disciplines.

Rather than studying biology, artificial intelligence, economics, or civilization separately, Connection Theory seeks the common organizational principles shared among them.

## Why Connection Theory?

Modern science has produced highly successful theories within individual disciplines.

However, many important systems—such as intelligence, life, artificial intelligence, and civilization—share remarkably similar adaptive behaviors despite belonging to different fields.

Connection Theory explores whether these similarities arise from deeper organizational principles.

## Research Scope

Current research includes:

- Foundations of Connection Theory
- Constraint Generation Theory (CGT)
- Flow Shaping Principle (FSP)
- Evolutionary Relativity
- Artificial Intelligence
- Collective Intelligence


## Current Status


Connection Theory is currently under active development through theoretical research, computational modeling, AI-assisted exploration, and continuous refinement.

The project is developed openly through a structured knowledge repository.

## About the Author

Connection Theory is initiated by Kai Wang, an independent researcher interested in intelligence, adaptive systems, artificial intelligence, and complex organizational dynamics.

## Looking Forward

Connection Theory is intended to evolve continuously as an open research project.

Research papers, technical notes, conceptual frameworks, and educational materials will be developed as the project grows.
