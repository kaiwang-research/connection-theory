# Theoretical Frameworks

## Overview

Theoretical frameworks extend the foundational concepts of Connection Theory into specific explanatory and analytical models.

Each framework focuses on a different aspect of persistent adaptive systems while remaining grounded in the shared conceptual foundations.

---

## Current Frameworks

The current theoretical frameworks include:

- Connection Theory (CT)
- Flow Shaping Principle (FSP)
- Evolutionary Relativity (ER)

Each framework addresses a distinct research question and is documented independently.

---

## Relationship to the Foundations

All theoretical frameworks are built upon the common conceptual foundations of Connection Theory.

The foundations provide the conceptual language, while individual frameworks develop specific explanatory principles, models, or mathematical formulations.

---

## Current Status

The current frameworks are actively being refined through ongoing theoretical research and will be accompanied by publicly available preprints.

As the research program develops, additional frameworks may be introduced.
