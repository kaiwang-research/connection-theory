# Connection

## Definition

A connection is a persistent or transient relationship through which components influence one another.

Within Connection Theory, connections enable organizations to emerge, maintain themselves, exchange information, transfer resources, and coordinate behavior.

---

## Intuition

Individual components rarely function in isolation.

Cells communicate through biochemical signals.

Neurons exchange electrical activity.

People cooperate through language and institutions.

Artificial intelligence systems exchange information through computational processes.

Without connections, components remain isolated and cannot form organized systems.

---

## Why It Matters

Organization depends not only on the existence of components but also on the relationships among them.

Connections determine how information, matter, energy, and influence propagate throughout a system.

Different connection structures can produce dramatically different organizational capabilities, even when the components themselves remain identical.

---

## Role in Connection Theory

Connection transforms individual components into organized systems.

It provides the structural basis for:

- Flow
- Constraint
- Feedback
- Adaptation

Changes in connection patterns often lead to changes in organizational capability.

---

## Examples

Examples of connections include:

- Synapses between neurons
- Communication between cells
- Social relationships
- Transportation networks
- Information exchange between AI agents

Although these connections differ physically, they all enable organized interaction between components.
